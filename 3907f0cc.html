Critique writing, 2

Using «that» or «which»

Is it a restrictive clause or non-restrictive clause? Comma is used to separate non restrictive clauses.

That
Brad’s sweater that has fancy elbow [restrictive clause] was a birthday gift from his sister.
Which
Stacy’s truck, which is painted red [non-restrictive clause], has a dent in the back bumper.

A comma after linking words

If a linking word start a sentence, they are followed by a comma. When they are used to connect two clauses, a semi-colon is used at the end of the first clause, and a comma is often used after the linking word (furthermore, however, indeed, etc).

Subject-to-verb agreement

First one has to identify the subject of the sentence, then decide if the subject is singular or plural, and finally decide which verb form will match the subject.

«A» or «an» before an «s»

If a word begins with a vowel sound, the indefinite article is an; it it starts with a consonant sound, the indefinite article is a. Thus, any word starting with «s» should be preceded by «a».

Run-on sentences

A run-on sentence occurs when two or more independent clauses (also known as complete sentences) are connected improperly.

Conciseness

Is communicating complete information about a topic or idea in a few words. Concise writing also involves being mindful of word choice. Limiting your word count is not enough to write concisely. You need to choose the strongest words to illustrate your point.

Tell the authors what do you expect

One has to be clear about what is the specific thing expected from them.

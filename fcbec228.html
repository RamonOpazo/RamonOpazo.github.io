Meiosis

Biological basis for diversity. We are sexually-reproduced organisms, thus, our cells are diploid. Only gonads produce haploid cells, process called meiosis. If the basis of individuality was based only on the fact of two progenitors, then why siblings are different?

DAN content

A premeiotic cell is diploid (2n). Meiosis starts with duplication of DNA (4c). Meiosis I ends up with a 1n/2c cell, and meiosis II with a 1n/1c cell. Thus, one DNA duplication and two succesive divisions.

Interphase -> Prophase I -> Metaphase I (where chromosomes are constituted in “huso meiótico” in homologous pairs, which have the same form and size).

Synaptonemic complex

The normal organization of the chromosomes in normal cells is as dense chromatin binded to the lamina. When the nucleus enters prophase I, the chromatin binds to an axis, except the telomeres which are still binded to the nuclear lamina. This is the leptoteno.

Then, the telomeres skid inside the lamina, finding the homologous chromosome. Zigoteno is the name when this synapsis starts, and paquiteno when the synapsis has been completed. This final structure is known as the synaptonemic complex (a proteic structure that bind two homologous chromosomes). The complex is formed in three parts: two axises from each crhomosome (lateral element) and one medial element. Participate in this bindind: SYCP1, SYCP2 and SYCP3 (mainly).

Durign the synaptonemic complex formation there is the highest trasncriptional activity of a cell. Also, there is expression of genes that are not expressed in other cells, and with an specific timing.

XY chromosomes case

The conduct of sexual chromosomes is different. When there is transcription in the autosomic bivalent, there is none in the sxual ones. If during pachytene there is no synapsis, then there is no transcription. But, this situation ins normal in the case for sexual bivalents.

The axis of both sexual chromosomes are separated except for a tiny spot where a nucleus is found. There is then no synaptonemic complex except in this part, where crossing over occurs. This region is called PAR (pseudo autosomal region). There is a newly described PAR2 region, small, with no crossing over. All these genes that are binded to chromosome X are not recombined and constitute the sex-linked genes.

SRY gene and chromosome Y

The SRY gene is deterministic of the gonad differentiation towards a testicle. This gene is located in the Y chromosome. This gene is located outside the PAR1, but is close to it. In some cases it can be transfered to chromosome X in faulty recombinantion.

End of prophase I

The system dissassemble except where crossing over happened (chiasma). The separation consolidates the crossing over in anaphase I. The origin of chromosome is defined then by the centromeric region. Anaphase II separates chromatids.

In oocytes there is an asymmetry in the distribution of cytoplasm, forming the polocyte and the oocyte in metaphase II. If there is fecundation a second polocyte is produced.

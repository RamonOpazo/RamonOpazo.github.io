This Zettelkasten implementation is a sistematization of notes and other related documents produced during the course of my Doctorate Program in Biomedical Sciences at Universidad de Chile. The program started on the first of March, 2021.

Organization of notes

The sistematization allows for three classes of relatedness between notes:

  • Parent notes introduce or enumerate following notes, called children. Any note can be a parent to another note, but in order to have a healthy heterarchy a logical relation needs to be assessed, so as to avoid many circular dependencies.
  • Children notes explain, further develop, or constitue an item of enumeration of a parent note. Any note in the heterarchy is a child from a previous more general one, except for the case characterized by notes that constitute the head of another heterarchy. Whenever a child note shares the same level in the heterarchy with another child note, they are commonly refered as sibling notes.
  • Reference notes correspond to ideas or themes that explain a particular topic in another note, but that fit in the heterarchy of that given parentageâ€™s note.

The case of train of thoughts

Whenever a Train of thoughts# is developed, the notes will be arranged in denceding order of parentage, regardless of how long such a train becomes. Sibling notes can be considered here as forking ideas born to the previous note. Train of thoughts should start a new, a can be referenced in any particular level, from any other note using the reference relationship.
